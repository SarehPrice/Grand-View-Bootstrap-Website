Review: It Follows. The new film “It Follows” has experienced about as much success as an independently made horror film could ask for. In its first couple weeks, writer/director David Robert Mitchell’s second film was screened in only 32 locations and had a planned video-on-demand release of March 27. After doing exceptionally well at the box office, the video-on-demand release date was pushed back indefinitely for the option to expand the film to more than 1,200 locations.

Public relations conference brings unexpected. From Friday through Sunday last week, the Drake University chapter of the Public Relations Student Society of America (PRSSA) hosted a regional conference. Short receives National Newman Civic Fellows Award

Human services junior Jess Short received the National Newman Civic Fellows Award for all of her leadership involvement. To qualify for this award, the student is chosen by the president from his or her university or college based on participation in civic engagement and contribution in the community.

Play Buffet: Comedy, Drama, Romance

Play Buffett is a compilation of seven short, entertaining plays students have produced and will perform starting at 7:30 p.m. on April 16 and 17. Overseen by Kristin Larson, professor of speech and theater, the plays range from romantic to comedies to dramas, but Larson said they are all entertaining.

Review: Lost River

Actor Ryan Gosling has stepped behind the camera for his new venture as writer/director for the new film “Lost River.” This fantasy-drama-thriller is about a single mother and her two sons. They live in a completely desolated area, and as Billy, the mother, does whatever she can to provide for her sons, her oldest discovers an underwater town that holds the secrets to the town’s problems.
